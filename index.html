Muhammed Ali

Muhammad Ali, boxing icon and global goodwill ambassador,

Some of Mr. Ali's achievements:

  • Mr. Ali was born January 17, 1942 in Louisville,Kentucky
  • He began training as an amateur boxer at age 12. At 18, he won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Summer Olympics and turned professional later that year
  • He won the world heavyweight championship from Sonny Liston in a major upset on February 25, 1964, at age 22
  • Mr. Ali dominated boxing in the 1960s and 1970s and held the heavyweight title three times. His fights were among the most memorable and spectacular in history.
  • Ali's actions as a conscientious objector to the Vietnam War made him an icon for the larger 1960s counterculture generation and he was a very high-profile figure of racial pride for African Americans during the civil rights movement and throughout his career. As a Muslim, Ali was initially affiliated with Elijah Muhammad's Nation of Islam (NOI). He later disavowed the NOI, adhering to Sunni Islam, and supported racial integration like his former mentor Malcolm X.
  • Outside of boxing, Ali attained success as a spoken word artist, releasing two studio albums: I Am the Greatest! (1963) and The Adventures of Ali and His Gang vs. Mr. Tooth Decay (1976). Both albums received Grammy Award nominations.
  • He also featured as an actor and writer, releasing two autobiographies.
  • Mr. Ali died June 3, 2016(aged 74) in Arizona
